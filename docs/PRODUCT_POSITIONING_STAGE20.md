# Stage 20 — Product Positioning

## Category we want to own

StratProof Lab should not compete as another trading bot, another generic backtester, or another strategy optimizer.

The category we want to own is:

> Evidence engine for trading strategies.

Alternative category phrases:

- Strategy audit engine
- Trading evidence platform
- Research quality-control layer
- Backtest truth system
- Strategy due-diligence engine

## Main positioning

Most trading tools help users build or run strategies.

StratProof Lab helps users decide whether a strategy deserves trust.

## Product promise

StratProof Lab turns trading ideas into audited evidence reports.

It checks whether a strategy is fragile, overfit, duplicated, regime-dependent, under-sampled, or misleading before capital is put at risk.

## Strategic enemy

The enemy is not another software product.

The enemy is false confidence:

- pretty equity curves,
- inflated winrates,
- cherry-picked charts,
- weak samples,
- same-candle fantasy fills,
- hidden duplicate clusters,
- overfitted thresholds,
- backtests with no out-of-sample discipline.

## Core message

Do not ask whether a strategy looks profitable.

Ask whether the evidence survives an audit.

## Differentiation pillars

### 1. Evidence before action

The system is designed to slow users down before they risk money.

### 2. Truth Engine mindset

The audit should actively look for reasons the result might be misleading.

### 3. Research University workflow

Ideas move through departments, not a single backtest button.

### 4. Visual credibility

The product must show the workflow beautifully:

- Formula Builder
- Evidence Cards
- Research Brain View
- Data Setup
- Provider Layer

### 5. Multi-market ambition

Crypto is the first market. The framework is designed to grow into forex, equities, futures, and institutional data.

## Homepage hero options

### Option A

Most trading strategies look good — until they are audited.

StratProof Lab turns trading ideas into evidence reports before you risk capital.

### Option B

Stop trusting pretty backtests.

Audit the evidence behind your strategy.

### Option C

## Best short pitch
